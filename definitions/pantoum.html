Pantoum

A Malaysian verse form comprised of a series of quatrains, with the second and fourth lines of each quatrain repeated as the first and third lines of the next. The second and fourth lines of the final stanza repeat the first and third lines of the first stanza.


Another Lullaby for Insomniacs by A. E. Stallings

Sleep, she will not linger:
She turns her moon-cold shoulder.
With no ring on her finger,
You cannot hope to hold her.


She turns her moon-cold shoulder
And tosses off the cover.
You cannot hope to hold her:
She has another lover.


She tosses off the cover
And lays the darkness bare.
She has another lover.
Her heart is otherwhere.


She lays the darkness bare.
You slowly realize
Her heart is otherwhere.
There's distance in her eyes.


You slowly realize
That she will never linger,
With distance in her eyes
And no ring on her finger.
