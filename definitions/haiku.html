Haiku

A Japanese poetry style that often is meant to depict the essense of a moment in time. In English it is usually written with three unrhymed lines of five, seven, and five syllables.


If Not for the Cat by Jack Prelutsky

If not for the cat,
And the scarcity of cheese,
I could be content.
